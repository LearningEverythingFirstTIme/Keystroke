How It Works

Four Steps.
Sub-Second Speed.

From keystroke to suggestion in under 300 milliseconds. Here's the pipeline.

01

Type

Type in any app. Keystroke listens system-wide via a low-level keyboard hook. Smart debounce triggers instantly at word boundaries, 100ms mid-word.

02

Filter

Sensitive data is scrubbed automatically. High-risk patterns block prediction entirely. Custom rules let you extend the defaults.

03

Predict

Your chosen AI engine completes the thought. Suggestions stream in progressively — no waiting for the full response. Cached predictions return instantly.

04

Accept

Tab to accept the full suggestion. Shift+Tab for one word at a time. Ctrl+Up/Down to cycle alternatives. Esc to dismiss. Keep typing to ignore.

The Learning System analyzes your patterns across apps — suggestions get smarter over time

[A] Acceptance Learning

Every time you press Tab, that's a signal. Every dismiss is one too. Keystroke builds a per-context library of few-shot examples from your real decisions — what you accepted in Slack is different from what you accepted in VS Code. Only high-confidence signals enter the prompt. Low-quality or ambiguous accepts are filtered out.

How it scores quality: 50% response latency (accepted in <300ms = excellent), 30% cycle depth (first suggestion taken = strong), 20% post-edit behavior (no backspace within 1.5s = confirmed).

[S] Style Profiling

Every ~30 high-confidence accepts in a category, an LLM analyzes your writing patterns and generates a natural-language style description. This isn't keyword matching — it understands that you prefer short declarative sentences in email but longer descriptive ones in documents.

Per-app categories: Email, Chat, Code, Writing, Search, Terminal — each gets its own evolving style profile injected into the system prompt.

[V] Vocabulary Fingerprint

A deterministic layer (no LLM needed) that analyzes your word choices, preferred phrases, and formality level. If you say "sounds good" more than 15% of the time in chat, Keystroke knows that. If you never use exclamation marks in email, it won't either.

Zero cloud cost: Runs locally as pure pattern matching on your learning database. Updated in real-time as you write.
